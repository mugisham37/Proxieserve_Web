The admin panel holds the most power, so it needs the most guardrails: confirm-by-typing on destructive actions, schema-publish migration warnings, append-only audit integrity, and permission boundaries even the owner can't cross.

03 / Trying to edit the audit log

"Even the owner can't"

Integrity
The audit log is append-only

Entries can't be edited or deleted by anyone — including you as owner. This is deliberate: the log is the source of truth if there's ever a dispute or investigation. Retained 7 years, exportable for legal requests.

Trust requires tamper-evidence. A government-adjacent product handling ID documents must be able to prove who did what. The audit log has no edit/delete affordance anywhere in the UI, and the backend rejects mutations cryptographically (hash-chained entries).
04 / Acting as an agent from oversight

"This will be logged"

Permission

Intervene on Aline M.'s case?

You're about to take an agent action (reassign / message client / change status) on a case you don't own. This is sometimes necessary — but it's recorded with your name, the reason, and a timestamp, and Aline is notified.

Oversight without surveillance creep. Admins can intervene, but never invisibly. Every privileged action requires a reason, notifies the affected agent, and writes to the audit log. This is what makes "admin → agent" a healthy relationship rather than a panopticon.
