Key design decisions

Three calls worth flagging.

Auth is the single place where the warm public brand meets the discipline of an internal tool. These choices keep the divide intentional, not accidental.

01 / Zone split

One system, two tones

Same tokens, same components — but the client side keeps marketing rail + italic-serif buttons + 440 px cards; the staff side strips to square corners + sans CTAs + role toggle + env badge. You should never wonder which zone you're in.

02 / Privacy by default

Never reveal account existence

Wrong password says "doesn't match" without naming the field. Reset-password says "if your email is registered we sent a link" regardless. Claim-by-code says "we couldn't find that" whether the code exists or belongs to someone else.

03 / 2FA mandatory for staff

No opt-out

Clients get OTP for first sign-in + new device. Staff get TOTP every sign-in with no skip — backup codes provided. Trust-device 7 days for staff (shorter than client) because agents handle other people's documents.
