Public tracker — edge cases.

The tracker is the most visited surface after landing — most ProxiServe applicants check it daily. These ten variants cover the moments where calm, accurate communication makes the difference between "I trust this" and "I'll just call them".

01 / Code not found

"That doesn't match"

Not found

That code didn't match.

Double-check the digits and dashes.

We couldn't find any application with that code. Make sure you copied it right — codes look like PRX-YYYY-NNNNN.
Lost your code?
Privacy first. We never reveal whether a code used to exist — that would leak account info to anyone guessing. After 5 wrong attempts in a row, we throttle to one attempt per minute per IP.
02 / Lost code — recovery flow

"Find my code"

Recovery

Lost your code?

Enter the phone you applied with — we'll send the code by SMS.

We send the same response whether the phone is registered or not — for privacy. Check your SMS within 60 seconds.

The recovery channel is the application channel. The phone you used to apply is the one that proves ownership. No password needed — the SMS is the proof. If they no longer have that phone, they're routed to support for manual ID verification.

06 / Status: Rejected — with reason

"Application declined"

Rejected
Identity & civil · Passport renewal DECLINED 3h AGO
PRX-2026-00483

Application declined.

Immigration couldn't process your renewal this time. It's almost always fixable — Aline has explained why and what's next.

Why it was declined

"Your passport photo doesn't meet the new biometric standard (ICAO 9303). The Immigration office requires the photo taken less than 30 days before submission. Yours was from January."

What now? Take a new photo, and Aline will resubmit at no extra service fee. Government printing fee (RWF 75,000) is non-refundable — Immigration's rule.

Upload new photo ↗ Talk to Aline → Request refund →
Tone for rejection. Lead with empathy ("it's almost always fixable"), then the specific reason in italic-serif (humanises a bureaucratic line), then concrete options. Government fees follow the office's own refund rules; we explain rather than apologise.
