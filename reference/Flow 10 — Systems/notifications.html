FLOW 10 / NOTIFICATION SYSTEM · 5 CHANNELS

One message, five channels.

Every status change, agent reply, and payment event flows through one notification engine that renders the same content correctly for WhatsApp, SMS, push, email, and the in-app inbox — respecting each user's preferences and the channel's constraints.

Where Rwanda lives. WhatsApp is the default channel — highest open rates, lowest cost, supports rich buttons. Uses pre-approved Business templates for the first message, then a 24-hour session window for free-form replies. STATUS / STOP keywords handled by a bot.
02 / SMS — the universal fallback

Same message, 160 chars

Fallback
PROXISERVE · 14:32
ProxiServe: Your passport renewal (PRX-2026-00483) is now In Progress. Track: prx.rw/t/00483 Reply STOP to opt out.
Reaches every phone. When WhatsApp isn't available (no smartphone, not registered), SMS is the automatic fallback. Composed to fit one segment (≤160 chars) — the engine shortens links and drops emoji. Sent via Africa's Talking with a Twilio failover.

For users who installed the app. Web Push (VAPID) to users who granted permission. Deep-links straight into the relevant case in F6. Quiet hours respected (no push 21:00–07:00 unless urgent action required). Falls through to WhatsApp if push fails.
