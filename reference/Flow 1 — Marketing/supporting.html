01 / How it works

From your phone to the government office, on your behalf.

Five things happen between you tapping "start" and receiving your outcome. We promise: no surprises, no hidden fees, no documents leaving the country.

01

You tell us what you need

Pick a service from our catalogue. Each one has a clear page explaining what document you'll get, what the government office requires, and how long it usually takes. Free to browse — no account needed.

Example — passport renewal
Fee: RWF 15,000 · ETA: 5–7 working days
02

You share what only you can give us

Your name as it appears on your ID. Your phone number. The documents the office needs — a photo of your existing ID, proof of address, your most recent payslip. Each one is encrypted the moment you upload it.

If you can't take a clear photo, we'll tell you and help you try again. If you don't have one of the documents, we'll tell you what to do.

03

An agent is assigned

Within one working day, a trained ProxiServe agent is assigned to your case. They reach out by your preferred channel — WhatsApp, SMS, or email — to introduce themselves and confirm the fee.

Every agent is ID-verified, bound by confidentiality, and reviewed by our team. You can see their photo and response time on your tracker page.

04

We apply on your behalf

Your agent fills out the official forms, prepares the supporting documents, and walks them to the right government office (or submits online where supported). You don't queue. You don't fill paper forms. You don't lose half a day at Kacyiru.

If the office asks for something extra, your agent will ask you for it through the tracker — you don't have to monitor email.

05

You receive the result

When the government office issues a result — approval, the printed document, a rejection with reason — your agent updates the status and uploads any official paperwork to your tracker. You get the file and the explanation in plain language.

If something can be collected in person, your agent does that too and arranges hand-off at our office or a courier delivery.

Account & access

Do I need an account? +
No. You can apply as a guest. We give you a tracking code (PRX-YYYY-NNNNN) and that's all you need to check progress. Creating an account is optional and helps if you have multiple applications.
I lost my tracking code. What now? +
From the track page, click "I lost my code". Enter the phone or email you applied with and we'll send the code by SMS or email. If we can't verify it's you, contact support — your agent can confirm your identity another way.
Can I share my code with my spouse? +
Yes, but treat it like a password. Anyone with the code can see your application's progress. For sensitive cases, we add a phone-verification step before showing details.

Applications

How long does an application usually take? +
Most services finish in 2–10 working days. Each service page shows the typical turnaround. If the office is slower than usual, we'll tell you in your tracker.
Can I cancel after submitting? +
Yes, anytime before we submit to the government office. After submission, withdrawal is sometimes possible — your agent can advise. Government fees may not be refundable depending on the office.
What if I started but didn't finish? +
Your draft is saved automatically on your device for 30 days. Come back to proxiserve.rw and you'll see a banner offering to resume. If you signed up partway through, the draft is also saved to your account.

Payments

How do I pay? +
MTN Mobile Money, Airtel Money, Visa or Mastercard. You can also pay your assigned agent directly — they'll issue a printed or PDF receipt. Off-platform payments take longer to confirm but are fully supported.
When am I charged? +
Never before we agree. After you submit, your agent confirms the final fee (sometimes lower than the standard rate). You only pay once you've approved.

Documents

What if my photo isn't clear? +
Our system flags blurry or dark photos before you submit. You can retake without losing the rest of your form. If your agent later needs a clearer version, you'll get a request through the tracker — no need to start over.
Where are my documents stored? +
On servers in Kigali, encrypted at rest. Only the agent assigned to your application can decrypt them. Documents are deleted 24 months after completion unless you ask us to keep them longer.

Privacy & security

Is my data really safe? +
Yes. Documents are encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). Access is logged. Agents are ID-verified and bound by confidentiality. We comply with Rwanda's Data Protection & Privacy Law. Read the full privacy policy.

Refunds

What if my application is rejected? +
If the rejection was an error on our side (bad form preparation, missing document), we refund our service fee in full. Government fees follow the office's own refund rules. Your agent will explain what's recoverable.
How long do refunds take? +
Mobile Money refunds: same day, usually within 2 hours. Card refunds: 5–10 business days depending on your bank. We'll confirm the refund in your tracker the moment we send it.

A small team in Kigali, helping people skip the queue.

We started ProxiServe in 2024 after spending too many days at government offices on behalf of family members who couldn't take time off work. We thought there had to be a better way. There is.

Make public services feel like any other service.

You should be able to renew your passport from your phone, on your lunch break, without asking your employer for half a day off. That's the goal. The technology to do it already exists — what's missing is someone willing to do the work on your behalf. That's us.

What we collect

In plain words: your name, your contact details, your ID number, and the documents the government office needs to process your application. Nothing more.

We collect personal information you provide directly during an application: your full name as it appears on government-issued identification, your national ID or passport number, your date of birth, your contact phone number, optionally your email address, and the supporting documents required for the specific service you've requested.

We also collect technical information automatically: the IP address you connect from, the browser you use, the timestamps of your visits, and your preferred language. This information is used to keep the service secure and improve it.

How we use it

To do exactly what you asked us to do — apply on your behalf — and to confirm it's still you when you come back.

Personal information is used solely to prepare and submit your application to the relevant government office, to communicate with you about the status of that application, and to verify your identity when you return to check progress. We do not use your information for marketing purposes. We do not sell or rent your data to third parties.

Who sees it

Your assigned agent. The government office your application goes to. Nobody else inside the company without an audit-logged reason.

Within ProxiServe, only the agent assigned to your case can decrypt and view your documents. Administrative staff may access metadata (status, timestamps, application code) for oversight purposes — every such access is logged and auditable. Outside ProxiServe, we share the specific information the government office requires to process your application, and nothing else.

How long we keep it

Twenty-four months after your application finishes, unless you ask us to keep it longer or delete it sooner.

Personal documents and application data are retained for twenty-four (24) months after the application reaches a terminal state (Completed or Rejected). After that period, we permanently delete the documents and personal details. Application metadata (tracking code, dates, status) is retained anonymously for legal and audit purposes.

Your rights

Access, correct, delete, or take your data elsewhere — any time.

You may, at any time: request a copy of all personal information we hold about you (data portability), request correction of inaccurate information, request deletion of your information (right to be forgotten, subject to legal retention obligations), object to specific processing activities, and withdraw consent for any processing that was based on your consent. To exercise any of these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer below.

What ProxiServe is

A platform that lets you hire a trained agent to apply for government services on your behalf.

ProxiServe is a digital intermediary service. We do not issue government documents. We submit applications to the relevant authorities and act as your authorised representative for that specific purpose. Approval of any application remains the sole discretion of the issuing authority.

Your responsibilities

Give us accurate information. Don't ask us to break the law. Pay agreed fees.

You agree to provide accurate and complete information, to upload only documents that are genuinely yours or that you are authorised to submit, to use the service for lawful purposes only, and to pay the fees agreed at confirmation.

Our responsibilities

Do the work competently. Keep your data safe. Be honest about what's happening.

We will assign a trained agent to your case, prepare your application with reasonable care, keep your information secure as described in the Privacy Policy, and keep you informed of progress and outcomes truthfully.

Fees & refunds

Our service fee is shown before payment. Government fees are passed through at cost. If we fail to deliver due to error on our side, we refund our service fee in full. Government fees follow each authority's own refund rules.

When we stop service

We may terminate service to any user who provides false information, attempts to defraud the government, or repeatedly violates these terms. We'll explain why and refund any unused portion of fees paid.

Disputes

We aim to resolve disputes informally first — contact support. If informal resolution fails, disputes are subject to the laws of Rwanda and the courts of Kigali.

We've designed ProxiServe to work for everyone — including users on low-end devices, slow networks, and users who rely on assistive technology.

Conformance level

ProxiServe aims to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA across all public-facing pages and the authenticated client experience. Agent and admin workspaces target Level AA with documented exceptions for dense data tables.

What works well

The full landing flow is keyboard navigable, with visible focus rings on every interactive element. All form fields have real labels. Status changes announce themselves through ARIA live regions. We respect prefers-reduced-motion and offer a high-contrast mode in user settings. Mobile touch targets are at least 44×44 px.

Known issues

The admin schema-builder drag-and-drop is not fully usable with screen readers below Windows; a keyboard alternative is available. PDF outcome documents are not always tagged for accessibility — we're working with the government offices that issue them.

Report a problem

If you find an accessibility issue, we want to know. Email a11y@proxiserve.rw or use the contact form. We reply within two business days.
