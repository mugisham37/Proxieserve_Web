Key design decisions

Three calls worth flagging.

Payments is where a beautiful product can still lose someone's trust in one bad moment. These calls are all about removing doubt.

01 / Mobile money first

Designed for how Rwanda pays

MoMo is pre-selected and listed first; card is third. The wait screen treats "check your phone, approve with PIN" as a real guided status surface, not a spinner — because that approval step confuses first-timers more than anything else.

02 / Never "was I charged?"

Every terminal state states the outcome

Timeouts and declines explicitly say "no money has left your account". Connection-drop mid-payment says "don't pay again — we're reconciling". The webhook, not the client, is the source of truth for success.

03 / Pay-agent, honestly

Support cash without hiding the trade-off

Many clients pay agents in cash. We support it but state plainly it's slower and unprotected until the agent confirms. The fee is always re-validated server-side at charge time so a stale amount is never silently charged.
