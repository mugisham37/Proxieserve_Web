Key design decisions

Three calls worth flagging.

These systems decide whether the product feels trustworthy in the moments that matter most — a stalled application, a dropped connection, a message at the wrong hour.

01 / WhatsApp-first

Meet people where they are

WhatsApp is the default channel because it's how Rwanda communicates — highest open rates, lowest cost, rich buttons. SMS is the universal fallback for non-smartphone users. Email is for records, not nudges. The engine picks the right channel per user automatically.

02 / Errors never blame

"Our fault, not yours"

Every error page reassures that data is safe, takes responsibility off the user, and offers a way forward. A 500 surfaces an incident ID to the on-call engineer. Offline still shows cached work. The product stays calm exactly when the user might not be.

03 / One system, every variant

Dark, RTL, print — token swaps

Dark mode, right-to-left, and print aren't reskins — they're the same tokens and logical properties resolved differently. The brand magenta and status hues hold constant everywhere, so recognition survives every theme, direction, and medium.
